# Audio: Checking Your Levels

Bad audio is the #1 reason a good piece loses points. Fix it before you shoot.

## Steps
1. Plug in the mic and put on headphones — always monitor.
2. Have your subject talk normally; watch the meters.
3. Aim for peaks around -12 dB to -6 dB. Never let it hit 0 (that's distortion).
4. Record 5 seconds of room tone (silence) for the editor.

## Common mistakes
- Filming in a loud hallway.
- Mic too far from the subject.
- Never checking headphones.
